What is it?

A Real-World CEO Experience

The ASCEND CEO Academy is a three-month applied leadership program where participants don't study executive leadership — they practice it, leading real organizations with real teams and real stakes.

Real Placement

Participants serve as acting CEOs or executive leads for partner organizations — startups, nonprofits, small businesses, or innovation initiatives.

Structured Support

Weekly reflections, one-on-one mentor meetings, and a just-in-time documentation library replace traditional lectures and coursework.

Executive Development

Evaluated across six CEO competencies — from strategic thinking and decision-making to communication and self-leadership.

"Leadership is learned through responsibility, not lectures — participants hold actual decision authority."

— ASCEND CEO Academy Core Philosophy
The Foundation

Five Operating Principles

Every design decision in the Academy flows from five core beliefs about how executive leadership is actually developed.

1. Leadership is learned through responsibility, not lectures

Authority first, curriculum second.

Participants must hold actual decision authority — not simulated authority, not advisory roles. This is the non-negotiable foundation of the program. Without accountability for outcomes, there is no real learning.

2. Reflection converts experience into leadership skill

Experience alone is not enough.

Weekly structured reflection is mandatory. Without deliberate reflection, leaders repeat mistakes rather than learning from them. The Friday reflection ritual is the mechanism that turns daily activity into compounding leadership growth.

3. Mentors serve as guides

Every participant has an experienced executive mentor.

Mentors are seasoned executives who review reflections, challenge assumptions, and provide coaching on decision quality. Critically, mentors guide thinking — they do not run the company. This preserves the participant's learning while preventing avoidable mistakes.

4. Documentation replaces traditional curriculum

A CEO survival manual, not a syllabus.

The Academy maintains a CEO Documentation Library — practical guides on firing someone ethically, managing cash flow panic, handling board pressure, rebuilding broken team culture, and more. Participants consult these only when needed. No wasted theory.

5. Problems drive learning

Curriculum evolves in real time.

When multiple participants encounter the same challenge, the Academy creates new documentation. This means future cohorts benefit from real problems faced by real leaders — and no learning resource exists until it's actually needed.

"Most leadership programs teach you what great leaders have done. We put you in the room and ask what you will do."

— ASCEND CEO Academy Program Design

12-Week Program

How the Program is Structured

Three phases take participants from orientation through active CEO leadership to final reflection and portfolio completion.

Executive Bootcamp Week

Full-time, in-person or hybrid. The single most intensive week — focused on mindset, systems, and preparation before participants step into their organizations.

  • Team formation and cohort building
  • Leadership mindset reset
  • Executive communication expectations
  • Program systems walkthrough
  • Reflection training
  • Mentor introduction
  • Organizational placement preparation

Participants Leave Week 1 With

  • Leadership journal started
  • Assigned organization
  • Initial CEO action plan
  • Defined success metrics

The Weekly Rhythm

Weeks 2–11 are the core of the Academy. There are no weekly lectures. Participants actively lead their assigned organizations on a structured weekly cadence.

Friday — Reflection Due

  • Key decisions made
  • Challenges encountered
  • People leadership situations
  • Failures and lessons
  • Questions for mentor

Monday — Mentor Meeting

  • Review reflection
  • Discuss decision quality
  • Leadership coaching
  • Challenge assumptions
  • Executive frameworks

Tue–Thu — CEO Execution

  • Running meetings
  • Operational decisions
  • Managing staff
  • Strategic planning
  • Handling crises

CEO Leadership Portfolio

  • Major decisions made
  • Strategic initiatives launched
  • Organizational outcomes achieved
  • Leadership mistakes and lessons
  • Personal leadership framework developed

Executive Presentation

  • What they inherited
  • What they changed
  • What worked
  • What failed
  • What they'd do differently

No Exams

Leadership is Assessed Through Action

There are no written tests. Evaluation is based entirely on demonstrated leadership across five dimensions.

01

Weekly Reflections

Every Friday, participants document key decisions, challenges, failures, and leadership situations. Reflection depth and self-awareness are evaluated over time.

02

Mentor Feedback

Executive mentors provide ongoing coaching assessments based on weekly meetings — evaluating decision quality, coachability, and leadership growth trajectory.

03

Organizational Performance Indicators

Real outcomes from the placed organization are part of the evaluation — did the team improve? Did the strategy deliver measurable results?

04

Staff Feedback

Where applicable, feedback from the teams participants led provides a ground-level view of organizational leadership effectiveness.

05

Final Executive Portfolio

A comprehensive document capturing the participant's full arc — decisions made, outcomes achieved, mistakes learned from, and their personal leadership framework.

"Evaluation should illuminate growth, not rank performance. Every assessment at ASCEND is designed to make participants better."

— ASCEND CEO Academy Assessment Design
